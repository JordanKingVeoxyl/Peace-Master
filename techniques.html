Techniques

CPR Meditation

Inhale: I am calm.

Exhale: Feel it.
Inhale: I am peaceful.
Exhale: Feel it.
Inhale: I am relaxed.
Exhale: Feel it.

3 Part Breathing

Breathe into your belly.

Breathe into your belly + chest.
Breathe into your belly, chest, &
shoulders

Inhale: 6, Exhale: 6

Inhale to the count of 6,

Exhale to the count of 6.

Body Scan

Slowly move your attention throughout

your body, beginning at your feet and
working your way up toward your head.
Observe any areas holding tension.

Square Breathing

Inhale to 4,

Hold to 4,
Exhale to 4,
Hold to 4.

Inhale: 4, Exhale: 8

Inhale to 4,

Exhale to 8.

Walking Meditation

Repeat this as you walk in a circle:

I have arrived
I am home
In the here
In the now
I am solid
I am free
In the ultimate
I dwell

Moving Meditation

Practice a form of yoga, tai chi, or qi

gong that connects your breath with
movement. Notice your sense organs response.
Every inhale and exhale, change your body's position.

Self Inquiry Meditation

(as taught by Sri Ramana Maharshi)

Ask yourself, "Who am I"?

Mindfulness

(Rest your attention on the present

moment.

Guided Meditation

Try following a guided meditation on

YouTube. You can also use apps such
as Headspace, Insight Timer, or Calm.

Mantra Meditation

On each exhalation, chant a mantra

such as OM or Shanti. (Shanti translates
to mean peace.)

Candle Meditation

Light a candle and focus your gaze on

the flickering flame.

Smile Meditation

"To meditate you only must smile."

- Eat, Pray, Love
simply: smile and evaluate how you feel.
Does it make a difference to your mood?
